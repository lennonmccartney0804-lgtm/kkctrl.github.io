01 Selected Work
2025
Infernal Hamlet
Writer · Director
A Hong Kong localization of Hamlet, reimagining the protagonist as Harper — daughter of Infernal Affairs' undercover officer Chan Wing-yan.
Short Film ~20 min Cross-cultural

A Hong Kong localization of Hamlet, reimagining the protagonist as Harper — daughter of Infernal Affairs' undercover officer Chan Wing-yan. Raised by the man who killed her father, she must navigate surveillance, inherited loyalty, and the slow collapse of everything she believed.

The adaptation strips out Hamlet's central question of revenge and replaces it with something harder: can you trust what you see? Harper doesn't dramatically delay — she accumulates doubt. A dream. A slip of the tongue. A photo turned face-down. By the time she knows the truth, the question is no longer whether to act, but whether she has any right to.

The film's emotional core is the relationship between Harper and Olivia — Ophelia reimagined as Hanzhen's daughter, who joined the police for love and hid her family not out of malice, but out of wanting to be seen as herself. Her death isn't caused by madness but by a fight Harper started. The first "To be or not to be" is Harper's breakdown after losing her. The second is voiceover over a blood-soaked document on a rooftop — addressed to the audience, unanswered.

Three characters, three irreconcilable reasons for being on that rooftop. No one gets what they came for.

2024
Blanche
Writer · Director · Full-cycle Production
An original French-language coming-of-age story about admiration, envy, and becoming yourself.
Short Film ~20 min French

Blanche idolizes her classmate Diane — until she discovers Diane's excellence was borrowed from someone else entirely. Years later, when Diane reappears genuinely transformed, Blanche must confront what she actually wanted all along.

The film's emotional core is a layered dream sequence: Blanche first imagines Diane during a group discussion, then falls asleep and meets her own internal version of Diane, who finally draws out the jealousy and desire Blanche has never admitted. The friendship is rendered in fragments — Diane pulling Blanche forward by the hand, teaching her French pronunciation, watching Chungking Express together.

Dreams by The Cranberries plays over these moments, chosen deliberately over the Wong Kar-wai version: a piece of Irish indie rock carrying the weight of a Hong Kong memory. Written and shot in French. Managed full production pipeline from script to post-production.

# Short Fiction
2026
Why Is Love So Embarrassing?
#multi-voice #zhihu-format #love #anonymous
Three anonymous voices on falling in love — structured as a Zhihu thread. A video game character, a best friend, a PhD boyfriend who never dresses up for dates.
Fiction

The extended exercise: write about the process of falling in love using mental, relational, and material processes. Rather than a single narrator, the piece uses a Zhihu-style Q&A format — three anonymous commenters, three completely different kinds of love, all arriving at the same conclusion about embarrassment.

The first commenter fell in love with a video game character and deleted the entire game trying to delete him. The second is a woman who only understood she was a lesbian the night her best friend's boyfriend came to dinner. The third stays in a relationship her friends find humiliating, for one reason: he will always welcome me home.

The piece ends with a meta-comment from 2026: all three voices were written by the same person.

2026
Fart Situation
#stream-of-consciousness #women #unsaid-things #montage
A class reunion, ten years later. Two women who have never said the right thing to each other, standing in a restaurant bathroom, trying again.
Fiction

Inspired by Horatia Ewing's style: a story where some truth of a relationship goes unspoken. Written using stream-of-consciousness and montage techniques — the prose fragments when the narrator's anxiety spikes, reassembles when she steadies.

The story traces a decade of unsaid things between two women through the texture of a single evening: the food that tastes wrong, the poem read in a stairwell that no one responded to, the moment someone says "we should be friends again" and the narrator thinks I never wanted to be friends with you but says something else entirely.

# Flash Fiction
2026
Annuata
#imitation #calvino #invisible-cities #memory
A city that only appears in spring. Written in the style of Calvino's Invisible Cities — addressed to an emperor, reported by a traveller who sneaks in during winter to find the truth.
Flash fiction

Annuata is a season city. Sometimes visible, sometimes not. A student remembers a swing near a kindergarten. An old man remembers only a railway. An office worker remembers both, vaguely.

The traveller sneaks in during winter and finds an abandoned kindergarten and a swing near the railway remains. The city is all three things at once, and none of them.
